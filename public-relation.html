Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between traditional PR and digital PR?

Traditional PR is exactly what it sounds like a classic, straightforward approach to PR that manages public opinion through channels like newspapers & magazines. On the other hand, digital PR makes use of more recent digital platforms like websites and social media to manage brand awareness while also influencing SEO & link-building operations.

What are the benefits of using a digital PR service?

Digital PR service enhances brand visibility through strategic online campaigns that help in reaching a wider audience. It provides measurable results & analytics, allowing for data-driven decision-making. Moreover, digital PR services facilitate media outreach, securing valuable press coverage. Additionally, it offers crisis management ensuring prompt responses to any online reputation threats.

What are the different types of digital PR services?

Digital PR is an umbrella under which there are a plethora of services including:

  1. 1. Mass press release dissemination
  2. 2. SMART PR
  3. 3. Social media management
  4. 4. Content marketing
  5. 5. Influencer marketing
  6. 6. Email marketing
  7. 7. Search engine optimization
  8. 8. Pay-Per-Click (PPC) Advertising

How can I measure the success of my digital PR campaigns?

Measuring the success of your digital PR campaign involves numerous crucial metrics. Start by tracking website traffic, engagement levels, and social media reach. Keep track of the number of press mentions, backlinks, and brand sentiment. Analyse conversion, lead generation, and customer acquisition rates. Finally, analyse the campaign's influence on overall brand reputation and visibility.

How much does digital PR cost?

The cost of digital PR in India varies according to numerous aspects, including the scope of the campaign, target audience, desired goals, and the agency or freelancer hired. Prices for small-scale projects might range from a few thousand rupees to lakhs of rupees for comprehensive long-term campaigns with considerable media involvement.
